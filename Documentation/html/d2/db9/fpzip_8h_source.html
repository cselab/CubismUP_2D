7 ** SUMMARY
8 **
9 ** fpzip is a library for lossless or lossy compression of 2D and 3D
10 ** arrays of single- or double-precision floating-point scalars.
11 ** Although linear (1D) streams of scalars may be compressed as well,
12 ** the algorithm has primarily be designed to exploit higher-dimensional
13 ** structure in the data, and may not perform well on 1D data. The
14 ** library supports compression to either a file or to main memory, and
15 ** allows specifying how many bits of precision to retain. (Currently
16 ** the precision is limited to 8, 16, 24, or 32 bits for floats and
17 ** 16, 32, 48, or 64 bits for doubles.) The decompressed data is
18 ** returned in full precision, possibly with the least significant bits
19 ** zeroed. Because floating-point arithmetic may be affected by factors
20 ** such as register precision, rounding mode, and compiler optimizations,
21 ** precautions have been taken to ensure correctness and portability via
22 ** a set of compile-time switches. For example, it is possible to specify
23 ** that floating-point operations be emulated via integer arithmetic, or to
24 ** treat the binary representation of floating-point numbers as integers.
25 ** Please consult the Makefile for choosing among these settings. The
26 ** compressor works correctly on the IEEE 754 floating-point format,
27 ** though no particular assumption is made on the floating-point
28 ** representation other than the most significant bit being the sign bit.
29 ** Special values such as infinities, NaNs, and denormalized numbers
30 ** should be handled correctly by the compressor.
31 **
32 ** fpzip was developed as part of the LOCAL LDRD project at LLNL, and may
33 ** be freely used and distributed for noncommercial purposes. The core
34 ** library is written in C++ and applications need to be linked with a
35 ** C++ linker. The library can, however, be called from C and FORTRAN.
36 ** For further information and bug reports, please e-mail pl@llnl.gov.
